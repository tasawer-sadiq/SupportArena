# Hackathon Idea: CivicConnect

## Idea overview
CivicConnect is a civic society platform where citizens can report local problems, propose solutions, and get those issues validated by open data. The platform helps community leaders, supervisors, and registered organizations act quickly on the most urgent civic problems.

## Core features

1. Citizen reporting
   - Anyone can submit a local issue, such as infrastructure damage, service outages, public safety risks, or civic problems.
   - People can comment and suggest solutions for reported problems.
   - Each issue can include location, category, description, and severity.

2. AI-powered validation and risk scoring
   - The platform uses AI to compare reported issues with open data and public reports from trusted sources.
   - It matches problems against relevant news reports, weather alerts, government open data, and disaster warnings.
   - The AI assigns a risk score and credibility level to each issue.
   - Problems with higher verified risk and community consensus appear more prominently.

3. Supervisor admin dashboard
   - A dedicated admin page for society heads and supervisors.
   - Displays high-risk issues, their risk scores, top community-recommended solutions, and status updates.
   - Allows supervisors to track open problems, assign priorities, and send requests for additional data.

4. Organization collaboration
   - Registered partner organizations can join the platform (e.g., hospitals, WAPDA, disaster management teams, NGOs).
   - Organizations see crisis-relevant issues and area-specific alerts.
   - Admins can send targeted requests to organizations for support, resources, or direct intervention.

5. Crisis notifications
   - The system sends alerts for upcoming or active crises such as storms, floods, heat waves, or other public hazards.
   - Notifications are based on AI analysis of open data, weather forecasts, and incident reports.

## How it works

- Citizens submit issues through a simple form.
- Other users suggest solutions and vote on proposed actions.
- The AI engine analyzes each problem by searching open online data and public reports.
- The platform calculates a trust score and crisis severity rating.
- Supervisors see the most urgent issues and recommended solutions on their dashboard.
- Partner organizations can review relevant crises and offer support.

## AI component

- Use AI to match submitted issues with open data sources, news reports, and official alerts.
- Extract credibility signals from multiple data sources.
- Calculate a criticality score for each issue.
- Recommend the top solutions based on community feedback and historical problem patterns.
- Optionally use natural language processing to summarize issue reports and map them to crisis categories.

## Why this matters

- Helps communities identify and respond to real civic problems faster.
- Improves transparency by linking citizen reports to verified open data.
- Supports supervisors and organizations with actionable intelligence.
- Encourages community participation and collaborative problem-solving.

## Benefits

- Better coordination between citizens, civic leaders, and organizations.
- Faster response to verified high-risk issues.
- Data-driven decision making for community action.
- Strong civic impact through a practical, focused platform.

## Suggested scope for the hackathon

- Build a working prototype with three user roles: citizen, admin/supervisor, and organization.
- Implement issue submission, community recommendations, and an admin dashboard.
- Add a simple AI validation step using open data matching or a rule-based model.
- Display high-risk problems and recommended solutions.
- Keep the demo focused and easy to show in a short pitch.
